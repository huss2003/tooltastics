## Why Two Systems Still Exist

The world is split between two major measurement systems: the **metric system** (centimeters, meters) and the **imperial system** (inches, feet). While nearly every country has officially adopted the metric system, the United States, Liberia, and Myanmar still use imperial as their primary system — and even metric countries encounter inches regularly when dealing with screens, piping, tires, and construction materials.

If you use RulerKit, you'll likely need both scales at some point. Understanding how inches and centimeters relate to each other makes you more versatile and accurate.

## The Centimeter: Metric's Everyday Unit

The centimeter (cm) is a fundamental metric unit for measuring length. Key facts:

- **1 cm = 10 millimeters** (mm)
- **100 cm = 1 meter**
- Based on the decimal system — easy to calculate and convert
- Used universally in science, medicine, engineering, and most countries' daily life

### Where Centimeters Dominate

- Global product packaging dimensions
- Medical measurements (height, wound size)
- School supplies and stationery
- European and Asian furniture specifications
- Scientific and technical drawings

## The Inch: Imperial's Workhorse

The inch (in or ") is the standard imperial unit for smaller measurements. Key facts:

- **1 inch = 12 inches = 1 foot**
- **1 inch = 36 inches = 1 yard**
- Typically divided into fractions (1/2, 1/4, 1/8, 1/16)
- Based on historical standards (originally the width of a thumb)

### Where Inches Dominate

- US construction and lumber dimensions
- Screen sizes (monitors, TVs, phones)
- Tire and wheel specifications
- Plumbing fittings and pipe diameters
- US clothing sizes
- Basketball hoop height (10 feet = 120 inches)

## The Conversion Formula

The exact conversion is defined by international agreement:

```
1 inch = 2.54 centimeters
1 centimeter = 0.393701 inches
```

### Quick Mental Math

| Inches | Centimeters | Approximate Reference |
|---|---|---|
| 1" | 2.54 cm | Width of a US quarter |
| 6" | 15.24 cm | Length of a dollar bill |
| 12" (1 ft) | 30.48 cm | Standard ruler |
| 24" | 60.96 cm | Average monitor width |
| 36" (1 yd) | 91.44 cm | Baseball bat length |

### Converting in Either Direction

**Inches to cm:** Multiply by 2.54

```
5" × 2.54 = 12.7 cm
```

**cm to inches:** Multiply by 0.394 (or divide by 2.54)

```
20 cm × 0.394 = 7.87"
```

## When Accuracy Matters

For rough estimates, remembering "1 inch ≈ 2.5 cm" works fine. But for professional work — especially in engineering, manufacturing, or design — always use the precise 2.54 conversion factor.

A difference of 0.04 cm per inch doesn't seem like much, but over 36 inches it adds up to nearly 1.5 cm of error.

## Which System Should You Use?

There's no right answer — it depends on context:

- **Use cm** when working internationally, in science, or with metric-native materials
- **Use inches** when dealing with US construction, screen dimensions, or imperial hardware
